Role of the Ah Counter:

The purpose of the “ah” counter is to make a note of the words and sounds used as a “crutch” or “pause filler” by anyone who speaks during the meeting. Words may be inappropriate interjections such as “and”, “well”, “but” “so”, “you know”. Sounds may be “ah,” “um,” “er” and so on.

 It is also necessary to take a note whenever a speaker repeats a word or a phrase incessantly, such as “I, I” or “This means, This means”, etc.

Your feedback to the speakers about their mistakes helps them recognize when they are doing them, why they are doing them and helps them improve upon those areas and their speaking delivery. You will probably find that it takes very focused, “active” listening to catch these speaking flaws!
